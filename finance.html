About Me

Hello! My name is Anjali Artz, I am a high schooler who is very passionate and focused on learning all there is to know about financial literacy and investing. While educating myslef about various financial topics, I love teaching my community aswell! Finance, specifically personal finance is something everyone should we educated on. We all use it in our daily life, and it is something that will continue to show up throughout all of our lives.

My journey to learning about financial education started when my dad first taught me about the stock market. We both sat in front of his work laptop, and studied how the stock prices would one day be up $30 and the next down $150. I was so confused and curious at thr same time at how people could just lose and then regain so mch money in such a short amount of time. I've continued learning about personal finance ever since, and it created such a strong bond between my dad and I.

My Financial Literacy Project: Empowering Peers

For my passion project, I wanted to teach many kids about finance, and how important it is. It is an important skill that many kids are not well educated on. I created a 20+ slideshow to showcase my understanding of financy, specificallly the stock market and investing. In addition to strenghtening my own personal knowledge, I wanted to educate my close friends and family about the topics I've been studying for as long as I can remember.

The Curriculum & Delivery

I created a educational and informational 20+ slideshow presentation, designed mainly for kids/teenagers with and urge to learn! I wanted the slideshow to be interactive, with many activites and Q&A sections, tending to each induvidual students needs. Topics covered included:

  • Understanding what the stock market is
  • Comprehending how you can NEVER predict the stock market
  • Bull vs. Bear market, and when the right time to invest is
  • How to diversify your stocks and create little risk
  • The importance of setting financial goals, and taking the proper steps to reach them
